<p><p>Allergy immunotherapy, commonly known as allergy shots, is a treatment method that helps reduce allergic reactions by gradually exposing the body to allergens. Subcutaneous immunotherapy involves injecting small amounts of allergens under the skin, while sublingual immunotherapy is administered as drops or tablets under the tongue. Both methods work by desensitizing the immune system to allergens, reducing symptoms over time. These treatments are typically used for allergies to pollen, dust mites, pet dander, and insect venom.</p></p>
