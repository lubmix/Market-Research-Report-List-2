<p><p>Fluid Catalytic Cracking (FCC) is a process used in refineries to convert heavy crude oil into lighter products such as gasoline. There are two main types of FCC market configurations - Side-by-side FCC and Stacked FCC. In Side-by-side FCC, multiple units operate independently next to each other, while in Stacked FCC, units are stacked vertically to save space.
