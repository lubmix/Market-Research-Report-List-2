<p><p>Grid-connected microgrids are categorized into three market types: AC microgrid, DC microgrid, and hybrid microgrid. AC microgrids operate using alternating current while DC microgrids use direct current. Hybrid microgrids combine elements of both AC and DC systems for improved efficiency and reliability. Each type offers unique advantages based on specific energy needs and local infrastructure. AC microgrids are commonly used for residential and commercial applications, while DC microgrids are ideal for isolated systems. Hybrid microgrids provide a versatile solution for a wide range of grid-connected environments.</p></p>
